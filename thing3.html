What is an Atomic number?

The number of protons in the nucleus of an atom, which determines the chemical properties of an element and its place in the periodic table.

What is an Atomic Mass?

The average mass of an atom of a chemical element expressed in atomic mass units. It is approximately equivalent to the number of protons and neutrons in the atom (the mass number).

What is a Group?

In chemistry, a group is a column in the periodic table of elements. Elements in a group have the same number of valence electrons, there are 18 groups.

What is a Period?

In chemistry, a period is a horizontal row of elements on the periodic table. Elements in the same period have the same number of electron shells, there are 7 periods.

What is reactivity?

In chemistry, reactivity is the impulse for which a chemical substance undergoes a chemical reaction, either by itself or with other materials, with an overall release of energy.

The number of valence electrons an atom has in its outermost shell determines its tendency to gain, lose, or share electrons with other atoms, thus dictating how readily it will participate in chemical reactions; essentially, atoms with incomplete valence shells are more reactive, while those with a full valence shell are less reactive.

What are shells?

Electron shells are layers around an atom’s nucleus that hold electrons. The valency is the number of electrons in the outermost shell, which affects how an element reacts. The period number on the periodic table shows how many shells an atom has. For example, elements in Period 1 have 1 shell, and those in Period 2 have 2 shells. The outer shell determines an element's reactivity.
